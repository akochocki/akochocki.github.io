Research

My work centers on mathematical models of branching populations with interactions, with applications in ecology as well as physics. I am particularly interested in:

  • Branching and tree-valued processes
  • Coalescent and genealogical structures
  • Interacting particle systems and formal frameworks for individual-based models
  • Adaptive network structures, transport, and interaction on networks
  • Scaling limits of spatiotemporal population dynamics
  • Statistical inference for complex and partially observed systems

Selected Preprints and Work in Progress

  • Threshold Resource Redistribution in Spatially-Structured Kinship Networks (under review at the Journal of Theoretical Biology). Introduces threshold-based redistribution dynamics on networks. Considers a two-dimensional stepping-stone population model with local interactions and redistribution dependent on agent genealogical (kinship) network structure. Focused on the relation between network topology, resource density, and population persistence.
  • Statistical Inference for a Point Process with High-Dimensional Observational Data (thesis section, work in progress). Develops inference methods for Poisson point processes under partial observability, motivated by point-source and diffuse analyses in high-energy astroparticle physics.
  • Machine Learning Processes for Observational Data (thesis section, work in progress). A discussion of generative and classification models for observational data, with emphasis on structure and assumptions.
